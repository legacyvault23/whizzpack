India is the world's leading producer of cotton, and its agricultural packaging industry has grown alongside it. **Cotton seed bags** made in India meet international standards and are used by seed companies, agricultural distributors, and commodity traders in the US, UK, Europe, and beyond.

This guide covers everything US and UK buyers need to know about sourcing cotton seed bags from India.

## What Are Cotton Seed Bags Used For?

Cotton seed bags are woven polypropylene (PP) or cotton fabric bags used to store and transport:
- Cotton seeds (primary use)
- Other agricultural seeds (mustard, groundnut, soybean)
- Animal feed and grain
- Fertilisers and soil amendments

The bags must be breathable (to prevent moisture buildup), strong enough to withstand stacking, and printed clearly for brand/labelling compliance.

## Types of Cotton Seed Bags from India

### 1. Woven PP (Polypropylene) Bags
The most common type for bulk agricultural use. Features:
- Weight capacity: 25 kg, 40 kg, or 50 kg
- UV-stabilised options for outdoor storage
- BOPP (Biaxially Oriented PP) laminated for moisture resistance
- Full-colour flexographic printing

### 2. Cotton Canvas Bags
Traditional fabric bags, increasingly popular for premium branding and sustainability:
- 100% natural cotton or cotton-polyester blend
- Breathable — ideal for long-term seed storage
- Reusable and biodegradable
- Popular for branded packaging in organic/heirloom seed markets

### 3. Multi-Wall Paper Bags with PP Liner
Used where regulatory requirements demand paper outer packaging:
- 2-ply or 3-ply kraft paper outer
- LDPE liner to prevent moisture ingress
- Common for retail seed packaging

## Key Specifications

When requesting quotes, specify:
- **Bag dimensions**: Height × Width × Gusset (in cm or inches)
- **Weight capacity**: 5 kg, 10 kg, 25 kg, 40 kg, 50 kg
- **Material**: PP woven, cotton canvas, or multi-wall
- **GSM**: 70–100 GSM for standard PP woven; 150–300 GSM for canvas
- **Closure type**: Heat-sealed, sewn, or valve-fill
- **Print**: Number of colours, pantone references, artwork files in AI/PDF
- **Certification requirements**: USDA, APHIS, or UK Plant Health

## Minimum Order Quantities

- **PP woven bags**: MOQ 5,000–10,000 units per design
- **Cotton canvas bags**: MOQ 2,000–5,000 units
- **Multi-wall paper bags**: MOQ 5,000 units
- **Container load**: A 20-foot container typically holds 50,000–150,000 bags (flat-packed, depending on size)

## Import Regulations for US & UK

### United States
- Cotton seed bags (HS 6305.33 for PP woven; 6305.20 for cotton) — duty rates vary; confirm with US Customs
- USDA APHIS may inspect bags used for plant material — ensure bags are clean and carry correct labelling
- California Prop 65 compliance if selling in California

### United Kingdom
- HS code 6305.33 (PP woven) or 6305.20 (cotton)
- UK Global Tariff applies post-Brexit; most PP woven bags from India attract 12% duty — verify current rates
- UK Plant Health regulations apply to bags that previously contained plant material

Work with a licensed customs broker in your country to confirm current duty rates and any phytosanitary requirements.

## How to Choose the Right Indian Supplier

A reliable cotton seed bag manufacturer should be able to provide:

1. **BIS/ISO certification**: ISO 9001:2015 as a baseline
2. **Export experience**: Ask for shipping records or bank LC experience with US/UK clients
3. **Testing reports**: Tensile strength, seam strength, and UV resistance for PP bags
4. **Printing samples**: Request physical samples showing print quality before committing
5. **Phytosanitary certificate**: If bags are made from natural fibres, this may be required for US/UK entry

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Suppliers who cannot provide physical samples within 2–3 weeks
- No willingness to do third-party pre-shipment inspection
- Pricing that is significantly below market (indicates substandard material)
- No documented export history
