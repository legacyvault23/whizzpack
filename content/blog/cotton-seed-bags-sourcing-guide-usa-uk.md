## What Types of Cotton Bags Are Available?

"Cotton seed bags" covers a wider range than most buyers initially expect:

**Drawstring pouches**: Lightweight, compact bags suited to retail seed packaging and promotional gifting. Usually 100–120 GSM fabric.

**Cotton muslin bags**: Versatile and breathable, these work well for seeds, herbs, and food-adjacent applications. Often unbleached for a natural appearance.

**Heavy cotton sacks**: Higher-capacity bags for bulk seed, grain, or agricultural storage. Heavier fabric weight (180–250+ GSM) and reinforced stitching.

**Custom printed bags**: Screen printing and embroidery are both widely available for branded retail packaging. Print quality varies significantly between manufacturers, always request printed samples.

## Is the Quality from India Comparable to Other Sources?

This is the question every first-time buyer asks, and the honest answer: it depends more on the manufacturer than the country.

India produces approximately 25% of the world's cotton. Gujarat and Maharashtra, in particular, have well-established cotton textile manufacturing industries that have been exporting to international markets for decades. The technical capability is there.

What determines quality is who you buy from and how precisely you specify what you need. A manufacturer who exports to the US, EU, and UK regularly will have calibrated their quality processes to international expectations. One who primarily sells domestically may not.

Key things to specify:
- **Fabric weight (GSM)**: Be precise. Ask for the actual weight, not a description like "medium weight."
- **Weave type**: Plain muslin weave for fine materials; canvas-style weave for load-bearing applications.
- **Stitching**: Double-stitched seams are standard for anything carrying weight.
- **Finish**: Bleached, unbleached, or dyed, and if dyed, what type of dye.

## Questions Worth Asking Before You Order

Here's what experienced importers ask before placing a first order:

**Can you send samples of the exact fabric weight and stitch I'll be ordering?** Catalogue photos don't show you what you need to know. Physical samples do.

**What's your minimum order quantity?** MOQs vary considerably. Some manufacturers accommodate smaller first orders from buyers who are likely to reorder; others have fixed minimums regardless.

**Can you work to custom dimensions?** Almost all Indian bag manufacturers produce to custom specs. Give exact dimensions and confirm feasibility before proceeding.

**Do you offer custom printing or embroidery?** Yes, widely, but request printed samples, not just photographs of printed samples.

**What documentation do you provide?** At a minimum: packing list, commercial invoice, and certificate of origin. If you need GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard) certification for organic cotton, ask specifically, not all manufacturers are certified, but some are.

**What are your payment terms?** Typically 30% deposit with the balance before or at the time of shipment. Terms vary by manufacturer and relationship.

## If Sustainability Matters to Your Brand

Cotton bags are often positioned as a sustainable alternative to single-use plastic. If your customers care about this, it's worth understanding what "sustainable" actually means in practice:

- **Organic cotton** is grown without synthetic pesticides or fertilisers. GOTS certification is the recognised benchmark.
- **Natural or reactive dyes** are relevant if your product is food-adjacent or marketed to eco-conscious buyers.
- **Unbleached fabric** has lower processing impact and is often preferred for natural product packaging.

Not every manufacturer can offer all of these, so identify which matter most to your use case before starting your search.

## Lead Times and Logistics

Production lead times for cotton bags are typically 3–5 weeks from order confirmation. Custom printing, embroidery, or unusual specifications add time; standard items move faster.

Sea freight from India: roughly 18–22 days to UK and Northern European ports; 25–30 days to the US East Coast.

For your first order, build in extra buffer. Once specifications are established and the supplier relationship is running, repeat orders become significantly more predictable.
