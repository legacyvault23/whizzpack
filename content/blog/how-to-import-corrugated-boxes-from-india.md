## Know What You Need Before You Start

Before approaching any manufacturer, you need to know what you're actually buying. Corrugated boxes have several key variables:

**Board grade (GSM)**: The weight of the liner and fluting layers. Heavier products need higher GSM combinations for structural integrity.

**Flute type**: B-flute is standard for general retail packaging. C-flute provides more cushioning for heavier goods. BC double-wall is used for heavy industrial applications.

**Box style**: Regular Slotted Carton (RSC) is the most common. Die-cut boxes allow custom shapes. Telescopic designs suit taller items or products needing tight lids.

**Print specifications**: Single-colour flexo printing is cost-effective for simple branding. Four-colour offset printing delivers sharper results for retail-facing packaging. Both are widely available from Indian manufacturers.

If you're unsure what you need, a good manufacturer will guide you, but you should arrive with a clear understanding of your product's weight, dimensions, and how the boxes will be used (warehouse stacking? direct-to-consumer shipping? retail display?).

## Flute Type and Board Grade Reference

Choosing the wrong flute type is one of the most common mistakes importers make on first orders. The table below outlines the practical differences:

| Flute | Height | Flutes per 30 cm | Wall Type | Common Use Cases |
|-------|--------|-----------------|-----------|-----------------|
| A-flute | 4.7 mm | ~33 | Single-wall | Fragile items, fine retail packaging |
| B-flute | 2.5 mm | ~50 | Single-wall | Canned goods, point-of-sale displays, inner cartons |
| C-flute | 3.6 mm | ~42 | Single-wall | General shipping cartons, produce crates |
| E-flute | 1.2 mm | ~90 | Single-wall | Small consumer goods, compact retail boxes |
| F-flute | 0.8 mm | ~125 | Single-wall | Premium packaging, high-detail printing |
| BC double-wall | 6.1 mm | B+C combined | Double-wall | Heavy industrial goods, dense export cartons |
| EB double-wall | 3.7 mm | E+B combined | Double-wall | Display boxes combining print quality and strength |

Board grade, specified in GSM, determines how much load the liner layers can bear. As a rough guide: 100 to 150 GSM liners suit light retail items; 150 to 200 GSM covers general shipping applications; 200 to 250 GSM is appropriate for heavier goods and multi-week ocean transit.

Specify both the flute type and liner GSM when requesting a quote. Manufacturers quoting on incomplete specs will often default to whatever they produce at highest volume, which may not match your requirement.

## Quality Standards That Actually Matter

For corrugated boxes, two tests tell you what you need to know:

**Bursting Strength (Mullen Test)**: Measures pressure resistance before the board ruptures. Relevant for boxes facing rough handling or compression.

**Edge Crush Test (ECT)**: Measures stacking strength. More relevant for warehouse environments where boxes will be stacked several layers high.

Ask any prospective manufacturer for current test reports. If they export regularly to the US or UK, these should be standard documentation. FEFCO box style codes are internationally recognised; specifying your requirement by FEFCO code eliminates ambiguity in communication.

## A Simple Checklist for Evaluating a Manufacturer

Before committing to an order, work through these:

- **Do they currently export to the US or UK?** Established export relationships mean they understand Incoterms, shipping documentation, and international quality expectations.
- **Can they send samples within two weeks?** Slow sample turnaround is a reliable early warning sign.
- **Are they specific when you ask technical questions?** Vague responses to spec questions usually mean vague production outcomes.
- **What's their process when goods arrive out-of-specification?** Ask this directly. A clear, fair answer indicates a professional operation.
- **Do their MOQs work for your volumes?** Minimum order quantities vary significantly between manufacturers.

## Don't Overlook Moisture Resistance

One thing UK and US buyers regularly miss: if your boxes will be stored in humid conditions, or shipped to humid climates, standard kraft liner may not be sufficient. Moisture weakens board strength over time.

If this applies to your use case, ask specifically about moisture-resistant board grades or wax-coated options. A good manufacturer will flag this for you, but only if you give them enough context about your storage and shipping conditions.

## Setting Up for Repeat Orders

Your first order from a new supplier will require more communication and longer lead time than subsequent ones. That's normal. Use it to document everything: exact dimensions, board grade, flute type, print files, finish, so that repeat orders can be confirmed against a clear specification sheet.

Importers who struggle with Indian suppliers typically skipped this documentation step early on. The solution is clarity at the start, not switching suppliers.

## Trade Glossary: Corrugated Box Terms for Importers

These terms come up repeatedly in manufacturer communications. Knowing them saves time and reduces the risk of specification errors.

**BCT (Box Compression Test)**: Measures the maximum load a filled, sealed box can withstand under vertical pressure before it collapses. Directly relevant to warehouse stacking capacity. Higher BCT means more boxes can be stacked safely.

**ECT (Edge Crush Test)**: Measures the ability of the corrugated medium to resist crushing when pressure is applied to the fluted edge. Expressed in lb/in or kN/m. A more practical indicator of stacking strength than Mullen for most modern shipping applications.

**FEFCO**: The European Federation of Corrugated Board Manufacturers. Their code system (for example, FEFCO 0201 for a Regular Slotted Carton) is internationally recognised and provides an unambiguous way to specify box styles across language barriers.

**GSM (Grams per Square Metre)**: The weight of paper or board per square metre of surface area. Used to specify both liner and fluting medium weights in corrugated construction. Higher GSM means heavier and generally stronger material.

**Mullen Test (Bursting Strength Test)**: Measures the pressure a corrugated panel can withstand before it ruptures. Expressed in PSI (pounds per square inch) or kPa. More relevant for boxes that face puncture risk during transit than for warehouse stacking.

**RSC (Regular Slotted Carton)**: The most common corrugated box style worldwide, referenced under FEFCO code 0201. All four flaps are of equal length and meet at the centre of the top and bottom faces when closed.

**Single-wall corrugated**: One layer of fluted medium sandwiched between two flat liner sheets. The standard construction for most shipping applications.

**Double-wall corrugated**: Two layers of fluted medium with three liners. Significantly stronger than single-wall; used for heavy goods, long-distance shipping, or items requiring extra protection.

**Triple-wall corrugated**: Three fluted layers with four liners. Used for extremely heavy industrial goods or bulk export applications where rigid box strength is critical.

**Die-cut box**: A box produced using a custom-made cutting die, enabling non-rectangular shapes, custom windows, or integrated display features. Higher tooling cost than slotted designs but more flexibility for retail packaging.

**Ply**: A layer. A three-ply box has one fluted medium between two liners. A five-ply box is double-wall. Seven-ply is triple-wall.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the minimum order quantity for corrugated boxes from India?**

MOQs vary by manufacturer, but 5,000 units is a typical starting point for standard configurations. Custom sizes, unusual board grades, or multi-colour printing often carry higher minimums because of tooling and setup costs. Some manufacturers will accommodate smaller trial orders for buyers who can demonstrate repeat potential.

**How do I choose between B-flute and C-flute for my product?**

B-flute provides a flatter surface, which is better for sharp print definition and for lighter goods that don't need significant cushioning. C-flute offers more cushioning depth and better resistance to compression, making it the preferred choice for heavier items or products shipped over longer distances. For anything above 5 kg per unit or requiring stacking of more than 6 layers, C-flute or BC double-wall is usually the safer choice.

**What's the practical difference between a Mullen test result and an ECT result?**

The Mullen test tells you how much pressure the board can take before it bursts, which matters for boxes that will be punctured or squeezed in transit. The ECT tells you how well the box resists edge compression, which matters for stacking. For most warehouse and retail supply chain applications, ECT is the more relevant figure. Ask for both; use them together to assess suitability for your specific conditions.

**Can Indian manufacturers produce four-colour printed corrugated boxes?**

Yes. Pre-print offset litho lamination is widely available and produces high-quality results for retail-grade packaging. Flexo printing directly onto corrugated is the more common option for standard shipper cartons where sharp print detail is less critical. Request printed samples before placing a production order, since colour output varies between facilities.

**How long does sea freight take from India to the US or UK?**

Transit times from major Indian ports are roughly 25 to 30 days to the US East Coast and 18 to 22 days to UK ports. Add 3 to 5 days for port handling at each end. Always confirm current transit times with your freight forwarder at the time of booking, as seasonal congestion and routing changes affect schedules.

**What shipping documents should I expect from an Indian corrugated box manufacturer?**

A standard export shipment includes: commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading, certificate of origin, and the manufacturer's quality test report. If your order includes custom-printed boxes, also request the print proofs that were approved before production. Keep these on file for customs and for specifying repeat orders accurately.

**Do corrugated boxes from India comply with US or UK packaging regulations?**

Indian manufacturers exporting to the US and UK are familiar with the relevant requirements, including ISPM 15 for wood packaging materials (relevant if wooden pallets are included), and standard customs documentation requirements. For food-contact applications, ask specifically about food-safe liner options and request the relevant documentation. Most experienced exporters can supply this; confirm before ordering rather than after.
